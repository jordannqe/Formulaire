To optimize your chances of winning a job interview, the Curriculum Vitae is an element not to be neglected. Traditional or original, it must be readable, concise, in agreement with the position Researched and regularly updated.

Our tips for shaping

  • Structure the layout in a clear way. Organize the data by headings (civil status, skills, experiences, training, interests interests ...) by aeration of the paragraphs.
  • Classify information chronologically, from the most recent to the oldest, or by function, from the most important to the least relevant to the profile sought. The second form being more suitable To an irregular professional career with off-peak periods (leave, unemployment).
  • Use a sober font (two different at most) using bold, italic and underline wisely.
  • Check the symmetry and regularity of margins, headings, paragraphs.
  • Check spelling and syntax. Use on-line correctors to help you detect faults.
  • Insert a photo of good quality, classic, well proportioned and correctly framed on a neutral background.
  • Reread the document by your relatives. External opinions will give you a critical look to improve presentation or content.
  • Send your resume by email in pdf format to be certain that it can be read by any type of computer.

Our tips for writing content

  • Summarize the information. The CV must fit on one page while remaining intelligible. Eliminate too old experiences if the document seems too long. Avoid repetition of technical terms.
  • Adapt and update the CV according to the application. Valorize the experiences that are related to the job and do not detail those that are not.
  • Mention your professional activities. (Date-company-function-grade). Use specific headings to describe your duties and responsibilities. Prefer the action verbs to the nominal groups.
  • Detail the tasks performed for each activity as well as the skills you have acquired if you do not have many professional experiences to your credit.
  • Provide concrete and / or quantified examples of the various professional goals you have achieved.
  • Specify your level of language and computer skills (mastered software and areas of use). At present, these skills are most important for most sectors.
  • Note your interests and hobbies, especially if they highlight your mental strength (high level sport), your team spirit (collective sport in association), your altruism (charity experience, Volunteering), your creativity (artistic activity) ...

Related documents

  • Accompany the resume letters written by your former employers which attest to your seriousness and abilities.
  • Add links that link to online folders, a specialized blog, or your profiles on business networks.
  • Include materials for the presentation of work in which you participated that may be of interest to future employers.
